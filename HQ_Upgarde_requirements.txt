Here is the comprehensive **System Portal Requirements Specification** broken down into functional modules, non-functional requirements, and database/architectural rules.
## 1. Functional Requirements by Module
### 1.1 User Authentication & Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
To enforce the required separation of duties, the system must support strict user permissions:
 * **Administrator / Owner Role:** Full access to financial metrics, banking logs, inventory controls, and system configurations.
 * **Administrative Desk / Cashier Role:** Permission to create invoices, accept payments, issue receipts, and run daily shift reconciliations. Restricted from altering job cards or accessing backend financial formulas.
 * **Service Technician Role:** Permission to create job cards, log hardware serial numbers, record diagnoses, and request parts from inventory. Restricted from accepting payments or issuing financial receipts.
### 1.2 Job Card & Workshop Management Module
To automate the hardware repair loop and enforce dual-custody rules:
 * **Job Card Creation:** A digital multi-part job card system requiring input of:
   * Customer details
   * Device serial number (compulsory field)
   * External physical condition (checklist/text)
   * Initial diagnostic notes
 * **Status Tracking:** Real-time state machine for job cards: Logged \rightarrow In-Progress \rightarrow Awaiting Parts \rightarrow Ready for Collection \rightarrow Paid & Released.
 * **Hardware Release Guard:** The system must programmatically block a technician or administrator from changing a job card status to Released unless a matching, fully paid invoice is linked to it.
### 1.3 Point of Sale (POS) & Invoicing Module
 * **Invoice Generation:** Ability to generate sequential, system-generated tax invoices combining service labor (e.g., laptop micro-soldering) and physical inventory parts.
 * **Payment Gateway Integration:** Interface to log card speed-point payments, cash payments, or EFT/bank transfers.
 * **Digital Voucher Sales:** A sub-module to generate, track, and log the sale of Omada network access vouchers.
### 1.4 Inventory & Tool Registry Module
 * **Asset Management:** A digital registry for specialized lab assets (e.g., multimeters, soldering stations) tracking serial numbers, deployment status, and depreciation.
 * **Stock Control (Ring-Fencing):** Inventory tracking for high-turnover micro-stock (e.g., thermal paste units, NVMe SSDs, RAM).
 * **Parts Authorization Workflow:** A system rule requiring technicians to input an active, verified Job Card or Invoice number to deduct stock from the digital ledger.
 * **Stock Auditing:** A "Physical Cycle Count" interface for monthly stocktakes that flags discrepancies between physical counts and the digital ledger.
### 1.5 Cash Management & Banking Reconciliation Module
 * **Shift Logs (Daily Protocol):**
   * *Morning Shift Open:* Forced input field to verify the opening cash float.
   * *Evening Shift Close:* A reconciliation interface prompting the user to input physical cash totals and card slips, automatically matching them against the day's system-generated invoice log.
 * **Multi-Stream Cash Flow Ledger:** Automated categorization of every transaction into three distinct macro-streams:
   1. *Operating Cash Flow (OCF):* Retail, labor, repairs, and recurring consulting retainers.
   2. *Investing Cash Flow (ICF):* Capital equipment purchases (lab tools, network masts).
   3. *Financing Cash Flow (FCF):* Owner capital injections, drawings, or debt.
 * **Digital Slip Upload:** A mobile-friendly interface or web portal feature to upload scans/photos of supplier purchase slips (e.g., from Pinnacle, Mustek, Communica) and link them to expenses.

## 2. Non-Functional & System Architecture Requirements
### 2.1 Offline Capability / Local Cache First
> **Context Note:** Designed for networks operating within logistically isolated regional hubs or rural nodes where internet connectivity can experience periodic downtime.
> 
 * The POS and Job Card modules must remain operational offline.
 * The system must use a local database cache to store transactions, syncing seamlessly with the primary cloud database once active internet uplink connectivity is restored or verified during the morning shift.
### 2.2 Security & Audit Trail
 * **Immutable Logs:** System logs for cash adjustments, invoice modifications, and inventory reductions must be completely immutable (delete/edit privileges blocked for non-admin roles).
 * **Data Encryption:** Sensitive financial records, client business data, and user passwords must be encrypted at rest and in transit.
### 2.3 Interface & Usability
 * **Clean Hierarchy:** A responsive web or desktop interface with clear scannability to minimize dense text overhead for administrative staff.
 * **Alert System:** High-visibility automated notifications for outstanding debtor invoices nearing payment terms and low-stock thresholds on core items (like thermal paste or storage components).
